K. M. ADIMOOLAM (1938 - 2008)

Add as Favourite

Born in Madras, K. M. Adimoolam is one of India's distinguished abstractionist painters. Adimoolam studied at government College of Art and Crafts, Madras, from 1960-66. During the 1960s, at a time of flux in Indian art when artists, institutions and artist collectives worked to redefine Indian art in an attempt to underscore an 'Indianness' in the face of the influence of Western art, Adimoolam pursued representational art. His distinctively styled sketches hold a forceful appeal, in particular those from the Gandhi series that earned him much acclaim.

In the 1970s, his monochrome sketches led to those drawn in bold colour bands, a change that led to his phase of abstraction. Adimoolam's works speak several languages, their appearance and treatment contemporary even as they draw inspiration from living traditions. His canvases, executed with floating colours formulating abstract imagery, lines, circles, zig-zag patterns, and shaded geometrical diagrams, like the cosmos or some distant planets, result from much experimentation. Within this abstraction is seen a reflection of nature and an attempt at a pure use of colour.

K. M. Adimoolam received the Bombay Art Society Award in 1965-66 and the national award by the lalit Kala Akademi, New Delhi. He has served as jury member for the national exhibition of Art, New Delhi, and Bombay Art Society's Annual All India Art exhibition, and as commissioner of the Indian section at the third Ankara Biennial at turkey in 1990. His drawings done between 1962 and 1996 were published in Between the Lines.
